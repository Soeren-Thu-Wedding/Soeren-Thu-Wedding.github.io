Phu Yen

Getting There and Away

Phu Yen is located about 1 hour away from Ho Chi Minh by flight and 3.5 hours away from Hanoi. As far as we know, there are no direct overseas flights to Phu Yen.

As such, we recommend first to book a flight from your home city to Hanoi, Ho Chi Minh or another major Vietnamese city. It might also be worthwhile to spend some time here before you go on with your journey. After that, you can book a flight from said city to Phu Yen (specifically Tuy Hoa Dong Tac Airport).

The two companies that operate flight to Phu Yen are VietJet and Vietnam Airlines.

From the airport, it is very fast to either get to the resort and hotel area in Tuy Hoa or to get to the village area. Both are about 10 minutes by taxi (in opposite directions). There will be taxis waiting at the airport when you arrive. The drivers probably cannot speak English, but if you just give them an address they should be able to figure it out. If you would like them to take you to Thu's parents' address, you can show them the following which roughly translates to "the furniture shop at Binh Hao, Da Ngu, Hoa Hiep Nam, Dong Hoa town (nearby the Hoa Hiep industrial factory)":

"Đồ gỗ Bình Hảo, Đa Ngư, Hoà Hiệp Nam, Thị Xã Đông Hoà (dưới khu công nghiệp Hoà Hiệp)"

Where to Stay

Below is a brief list of recommendations on places to stay in Phu Yen. Most places are relatively cheap.

Kaya Hotel The Kaya Hotel is conveniently located just off the highway and close to the wedding venue. It is about a 20 minute drive to downtown Phu Yen. It is a decent hotel that is fairly priced.
Rosa Alba Resort & Villa The Rosa Alba Resort & Villa is close to the Kaya Hotel. It is a very nice resort that is somewhat more expensive than Kaya Hotel. If you go in a large group, you can also book a villa here that can house up to seven people. Their promotional video shows off the resort quite nicely.
Hotels/Bed and Breakfasts in Town There are a large number of smaller hotels and B&B's in and around Tuy Hoa. We recommend just jumping on Tripadvisor or your favorite travel website and choosing a place.
Vacation rentals You can of course always use Airbnb if you don't mind something a bit more self-service.

Places to Visit

We encourage you to utilize your Vietnam trip for more than just spending time in Phu Yen. You should also enjoy Hanoi, Ho Chi Minh, Da Nang wherever you arrive first. Having said that, when you do land in Phu Yen, some ideas for things to do are listed below.

Mui Dien Lighthouse

Very nearby to Thu's parents' place with just a 15 min scooter ride. You will be amazed by how beautiful the scenery is where the mountain meets the sea. It's where you can catch the first sunlight on land in Vietnam (easternmost part of Vietnam).

Da Dia Reef

One of only four hexagon-formed reefs in the world. Tucked away at the end of An Ninh Dong commune, Tuy An district, Da Dia reef is a beguiling natural wonder of Phu Yen with black basalt rock formations rising along the sea. Countless black polygonal rocks stacking up as a result of volcanic eruptions that took place millions of years ago. They form a pitch dark seaside wall that looks like piles of plates.

Tuy Hoa Beach

There are many beach resorts, cafes, and restaurants around Tuy Hoa beach that you can explore. But it's not recommended to swim in October due to big waves and windy weather during the raining season.

Nhan Temple

As the last relic of the Champa Kingdom, Nhan Temple is an important symbol of Phu Yen's historical values and interesting anecdotes. Located close to Nhan Mountain, on a hilltop of the northern bank of Da Rang River.

Vung Ro Bay

It is located just 5 km away from Mui Dien Lighthouse, you can combine the two locations in a day. Here you can try local seafood like lobsters, prawns, cockles, and fish on the spot, and dive to see beautiful corals.
